Sydney-founded beauty-tech startup Aglow has closed a $1.25 million seed round as its membership model for salons and clinics gains momentum across Australia and New Zealand.

The round was backed by a group of operators that includes the founders of payments company Tyro and salon-software business Timely — investors with deep roots in the small-business and beauty-services markets Aglow is targeting.

Aglow's platform lets beauty and aesthetics businesses offer recurring memberships to their clients, replacing one-off appointment bookings with subscription plans that smooth revenue and improve retention. The company says memberships give clinics more predictable income while giving clients a simpler way to budget for regular treatments.

A recurring-revenue bet on beauty

The membership model is well established in fitness and wellness, but Aglow is betting it can reshape a beauty-services sector that the company pegs at more than $11 billion in Australia alone. Early adopters span skin clinics, injectable providers and salons looking to lock in repeat custom.

The fresh capital will go toward product development and expanding Aglow's footprint with clinics in both Australian and New Zealand markets, the company said.
